# Ui Deslop

## Trigger

- A design uses predictable gradient heroes, centered stacks, card grids, pills, or decorative glow.
- Any interface-and-experience skill is preparing final output.

## Inputs

- Brief and audience
- Existing interface or design
- Brand/product constraints
- Accessibility requirements

## Procedure

1. Name the interface genre and user job before styling.
2. Identify the structural fingerprint: hero, navigation, section rhythm, feature presentation, proof, CTA, and footer.
3. Flag AI-default patterns: purple/pink gradient hero, centered-everything composition, generic icon tiles, excessive rounded cards/pills, glassmorphism, ambient blobs, uniform 3-column grids, fake metrics, and default SaaS copy.
4. Change macrostructure before surface decoration.
5. Choose one anchor hue, restrained accents, a clear type hierarchy, and a named spacing scale.
6. Use asymmetry, editorial rhythm, product evidence, and content-specific components where they clarify the experience.
7. Remove decoration that lacks semantic or interaction value.
8. Re-run accessibility, responsive, and artifact-delint gates.

## Output contract

- Reworked interface/design specification
- Before/after anti-pattern register
- Mandatory pass/fail deslop score

## Quality gates

- No fabricated metrics, testimonials, logos, or product proof.
- The result has a distinct structural fingerprint.
- Accent, radius, shadow, and motion are restrained and coherent.
- Function and accessibility are not traded for novelty.

## Handoffs and dependencies

- `hallmark-upstream/repository/skills/hallmark`
- `design-pattern-detector`
- `artifact-delint`
- `interface-and-experience/*`

## Boundaries

- Do not make every layout asymmetric regardless of task.
- Do not remove established brand patterns without reason.
- Do not imitate a reference pixel-for-pixel or copy paid templates.
